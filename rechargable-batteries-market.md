<p><p>The rechargeable batteries market encompasses various types based on chemistry and application. Ni-Cd (Nickel-Cadmium) batteries are known for their durability and high discharge rates but have environmental concerns. Ni-MH (Nickel-Metal Hydride) batteries offer improved energy density and are commonly used in consumer electronics. Li-Ion (Lithium-Ion) batteries are favored for their high energy capacity, lightweight design, and efficiency, making them prevalent in portable devices and electric vehicles. Other battery types include Lithium Polymer and lead-acid variants, catering to specific needs and applications.</p></p>
